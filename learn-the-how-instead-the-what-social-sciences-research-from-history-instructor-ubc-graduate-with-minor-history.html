Learn the "how" instead of the "what" of social sciences research from history instructor, UBC graduate with minor in history

Methodology

The humanities curriculum that students follow at school often focuses on teaching the "what" -- what happened in December of 1773 (Boston tea party?) -- instead of the “how,” as in, how should you write an A-level history research paper? This is the gap I aim to fill with my instruction. With my undergraduate training in historical research and analysis, I can share the "how" side of the equation so students are more confident in their approach to writing essays in the social sciences.

I constantly strive to bring in new material for lessons. Based on the student's needs, I will either give an introduction or assign readings to the student to help establish a basic understanding of the subject. Then, we will engage with a variety of primary and secondary sources to deepen our understanding of the subject and practice historical analysis. The methodology varies based on the student's learning preferences, but my prioritizing of helping the student develop critical thinking and analytical skills remains a constant.

For IHBB (history bee and bowl) students, get in touch for more details.

Ambassador

One of our best! High-quality profile, qualifications verified and responds during the day. Michelle will plan your first lesson with care.

Background

I am a UBC graduate with 5 years teaching experience in middle and high school humanities and science. I currently hold a BA in English literature and a minor in history with a focus on modern China. I have a deep understanding of the discipline and understand what instructors want in essays and projects.

I have worked with many middle and high school students on history and social studies in the past. I am familiar with the BC curriculum and have created full courses that help students reach their learning goals. Feel free to schedule a free initial meeting to discuss goals and expectations.
